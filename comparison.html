From Parks to Prices: Greenspace Inequity

Relating Housing Price to Greenspace Access and Quality

Findings: Park Ratings vs Housing Price

Districts with higher housing prices tend to enjoy higher park ratings. Conversely, many lower-priced areas suffer from relatively poor park satisfaction, highlighting a spatial disparity in greenspace quality. Additionally, we identified two districts in Manhattan where high housing prices coexist with low park ratings. These areas are located near Central Park and, despite being adjacent to high-quality green resources, the internal greenspace quality of the districts themselves still needs further improvement. Findings: Park Accessibility vs Housing Price

In contrast, districts like BX10 and QE08 exhibit high rental prices but low park service rates, indicating underserved areas despite their higher housing costs.

Policy Recommendations

To promote a more equitable city, it is essential for New York City to invest in upgrading greenspaces in underserved areas. This includes enhancing park facilities, increasing park accessibility, and involving local communities in the planning and management of urban green infrastructure.
